Project 2: Seattle Reptile Expo


Developing a Brand Identity

This personal project started as a poster design for a fake event that could also be used for other promotional products. I chose to do a Seattle Reptile Expo.

I first researched reptiles that are native to the PNW for inspiration. I chose the pacific ring-necked snake, due to its striking red and black pattern. ‍

The illustration is simple and graphic. I wanted the shape and color to be the main focus. The snake can be altered in different lengths to fit different marketing materials.

Overall I wanted the poster to have a professional look for the event, but also be family-friendly and welcoming. Using minimal but bold colors keeps the design clean, and the snake graphic helps grab your attention.

Developing a Website

After creating the event’s marketing materials, I wanted to create a mockup of what the website might look like for this event. I incorporated many elements from the original poster design, including the winding black snake, dark greys, and a similar font. I selected colorful photos of reptiles and put a slight red overlay on them to unify the look.

Finally, I wanted to create a working example of how the winding snake would work using HTML and CSS. I first made a 3 column flexbox to hold the whole "snake". I then placed in the 2nd column a div container with a grid. This allowed me to place the bends of the snake. The bends were then created using two divs stacked on each other, and using a curved radius to give the illusion that the snake is all connected.
